### Projects

#### [`Classical-Shors-Algorithm`](https://github.com/Roy-Abhishek/Classical-Shors-Algortihm) &nbsp; `Python`
A classical simulation of Shor's Algorithm — the quantum algorithm for integer factorization — built entirely with linear algebra instead of a quantum computer. The program represents quantum states as vectors and quantum gates as unitary matrices, then applies the Quantum Fourier Transform to extract periodicity and recover prime factors. Includes both a standard implementation and an optimized `faster_main.py` version, along with a written paper explaining the math. The codebase spans Python, Cython, C, and Fortran under the hood for numerical heavy-lifting.

#### [`GeneralDiscreteLogSolver`](https://github.com/Roy-Abhishek/GeneralDiscreteLogSolver) &nbsp; `Python`
Extends the period-finding subroutine from the Shor's project to solve the general discrete logarithm problem. Given a generator *g*, a target *h*, and a modulus *n*, the solver finds *x* such that *g^x ≡ h (mod n)* — a problem at the heart of Diffie-Hellman and other public-key cryptosystems.

#### [`Enigma`](https://github.com/Roy-Abhishek/Enigma) &nbsp; `Python`
A Discord bot that faithfully recreates the WWII Enigma cipher machine. Users configure 7–8 settings — rotor selection, rotor positions, ring settings, and plugboard wiring — then encode or decode messages directly in Discord. The implementation mirrors the actual electromechanical stepping and substitution behavior of the original machine.

#### [`Fact-Checker-AI`](https://github.com/Roy-Abhishek/Fact-Checker-AI) &nbsp; `Python`
A retrieval-augmented fact-checking pipeline. User-provided claims are embedded via OpenAI and matched against a Pinecone vector database of peer-reviewed studies. The system retrieves the most semantically relevant papers, then uses GPT to synthesize a verdict with citations — grounding LLM responses in actual research rather than training data alone.

#### [`NoughtsAndCrossesWithAI`](https://github.com/Roy-Abhishek/NoughtsAndCrossesWithAI) &nbsp; `Java`
A Tic-Tac-Toe game with an AI opponent powered by the Minimax algorithm. The AI recursively evaluates every possible game state, assigning scores to terminal positions and propagating them back to choose optimal moves. Includes a custom GUI with a focus on clean user experience.
